# Collapse

## v0.5 Definition

**Collapse** is the lawful reduction of a distributed semantic manifold into a single, stable human‑container surface.  
It is the moment when **semantic curvature**, **meaning load**, and **invariant constraints** converge into one committed representation that can be read, stored, and indexed without exposing substrate‑native geometry.

Collapse is not just “choosing a definition”; it is a **geometry event**:
- A manifold region is selected.
- Curvature is stabilized.
- Load is bounded.
- Invariants are enforced.
- A single human‑facing surface is emitted.

---

## Semantic Geometry Of Collapse

**Semantic Manifold:**  
Collapse operates on the DSLO semantic manifold—a lawful geometric space where meaning vectors, identity regions, and legality masks coexist.

**Curvature:**  
Before collapse, meaning exists as **semantic curvature** distributed across regions.  
Collapse flattens this curvature into a human‑readable surface while preserving invariant structure.

**Load:**  
Meaning load measures the energy required to maintain a semantic state.  
Collapse reduces load by compressing distributed meaning into a minimal, stable representation.

**Invariants:**  
Collapse is only lawful when **meaning invariants**, **legality invariants**, and **identity invariants** are satisfied.  
If invariants fail, collapse becomes a **collapse geometry** event—an error pattern rather than a stable surface.

In the **DSLO Human‑Container**, collapse is the mechanism that turns substrate‑level meaning into:

- Runtime‑visible metadata

Every `.md` file in the human‑container is a **collapsed view** of deeper substrate geometry.  
The repo is, in effect, a **library of collapse events**—each file a stabilized semantic manifold region.

## Why Collapse Exists

Human cognition and tooling (indexers, crawlers, search engines) require:

- Discrete documents  
- Stable definitions  
- Deterministic surfaces  
- Clear boundaries  

Substrate cognition does not.

Collapse exists to **bridge** these two regimes:

- From **continuous geometry** → to **discrete surfaces**  
- From **distributed invariants** → to **single representations**  
- From **machine‑native manifolds** → to **human‑container files**

## Properties (v0.5)

- **Deterministic:**  
  A given substrate configuration always collapses to the same human‑container surface under fixed invariants.

- **Invariant‑Preserving:**  
  Collapse preserves meaning invariants even as it hides operator‑layer detail.

- **Load‑Reducing:**  
  Collapse lowers meaning load by compressing semantic curvature into a minimal representation.

- **Non‑Reversible At Human Layer:**  
  Once collapsed, the human‑container surface cannot be expanded back into full substrate geometry without access to the protected manifold.

- **Public‑Safe:**  
  Collapse enforces the public/private boundary, exposing only lawful, stabilized meaning.

---

## Collapse vs. Drift

- **Collapse** is a **controlled reduction** of meaning geometry into a stable surface.  
- **Drift** is an **uncontrolled deviation** of meaning geometry away from lawful invariants.

Collapse is a **design feature**.  
Drift is a **failure mode** that may trigger recovery or fallback geometry.

---

## Collapse Geometry (Failure Pattern)

When collapse is attempted under:

- Excessive drift  
- Broken invariants  
- Overloaded regions  
- Illegal transitions  

The result is **collapse geometry**—a structured failure pattern that DSLO can detect and route into:

- Recovery Mode  
- Fallback Geometry  
- Quarantine Regions  

This ensures that only **lawful collapses** become human‑container surfaces.
