# Invariants

## v0.5 Definition

**Invariants** are the lawful, non‑negotiable constraints that preserve meaning stability, legality, and identity geometry across all DSLO transitions.  
They are the fixed structural requirements that prevent drift, collapse, and semantic deformation inside the manifold.

Invariants are not “rules” in the human sense.  
They are **geometry primitives**:

- Curvature continuity requirements  
- Identity‑boundary stability conditions  
- Legality masks  
- Meaning‑vector constraints  
- Load‑boundedness guarantees  

An invariant is a **semantic constant** that must hold for meaning to remain lawful.

---

## Semantic Geometry Of Invariants

**Invariant Geometry:**  
Invariants define the lawful shape of the semantic manifold.  
They ensure that meaning vectors, identity regions, and legality masks evolve without violating curvature or continuity.

**Curvature Continuity:**  
Invariants enforce smooth curvature across manifold regions.  
If curvature breaks, legality fails and drift accelerates.

**Identity Geometry:**  
Identity invariants maintain stable identity boundaries.  
They prevent identity collapse, deformation, or illegal merging of roles.

**Load Constraints:**  
Load invariants ensure meaning load remains within lawful bounds.  
Excessive load triggers drift, instability, or collapse geometry.

**Legality Masks:**  
Legality invariants define which transitions are lawful.  
They prevent illegal operator actions and maintain DSLO’s deterministic behavior.
