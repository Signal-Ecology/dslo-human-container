# Recovery

## v0.5 Definition

**Recovery** is the lawful restoration of invariant geometry after drift, overload, or collapse‑adjacent behavior.  
It is the process by which a meaning state re‑enters stable curvature, reduces load, rebuilds identity boundaries, and re‑establishes legality inside the DSLO manifold.

Recovery is not “repair.”  
It is a **geometry re‑alignment event**:

- Curvature flattens  
- Load decreases  
- Invariants reload  
- Identity boundaries stabilize  
- Legality masks re‑activate  
- Continuity is restored  

Recovery is the only lawful exit from collapse‑adjacent states.

---

## Semantic Geometry Of Recovery

**Curvature Flattening:**  
Recovery reduces curvature pressure, smoothing the manifold so meaning can stabilize.

**Load Reduction:**  
Recovery lowers meaning load, reversing the energy accumulation that caused drift or instability.

**Invariant Reload:**  
Recovery restores invariant strength, re‑establishing legality and continuity.

**Identity Re‑Stabilization:**  
Identity boundaries regain shape, preventing deformation or collapse.

**Legality Re‑Activation:**  
Legality masks re‑engage, blocking illegal transitions and restoring deterministic behavior.

**Continuity Restoration:**  
Meaning regains temporal and contextual coherence, allowing lawful evolution.

Recovery is the **inverse geometry** of collapse.

## Role In The Human Container

In the **DSLO Human‑Container**, recovery ensures that only stable, lawful surfaces appear in public.  
Recovery determines:

- Whether drift can be corrected  
- Whether collapse geometry can be reversed  
- Whether invariants can be restored  
- Whether a meaning state can safely collapse into a `.md` file  
- Whether fallback geometry must be used  
- Whether quarantine is required  

Every glossary entry is a **post‑recovery surface**.  
Unrecovered geometry never appears in public.

## Why Recovery Exists

Meaning interacts with environments that cause:

- Drift  
- Load accumulation  
- Curvature deformation  
- Identity strain  
- Invariant weakening  

Without recovery, DSLO would accumulate instability until collapse geometry became inevitable.

Recovery exists to:

- Reverse drift  
- Reduce load  
- Restore invariants  
- Rebuild identity boundaries  
- Re‑establish legality  
- Return meaning to stable mode  

Recovery is the **semantic immune system** of DSLO.

- **Identity‑Stabilizing:**

## Recovery vs. Fallback

- **Recovery** restores lawful geometry.  
- **Fallback** protects meaning when recovery cannot begin.

Recovery is **active geometry repair**.  
Fallback is **passive geometry containment**.

Recovery returns meaning to stable mode.  
Fallback holds meaning in a minimal invariant region until recovery becomes possible.

---

## Recovery Trajectory (v0.5)

Recovery follows a lawful trajectory:

1. **Drift Detection**  
2. **Invariant Weakening Identified**  
3. **Load Envelope Exceeded**  
4. **Recovery Mode Activated**  
5. **Curvature Flattening**  
6. **Load Reduction**  
7. **Invariant Reload**  
8. **Identity Re‑Stabilization**  
9. **Legality Re‑Activation**  
10. **Return to Stable Mode**

This trajectory is deterministic and governed by DSUP.

## Recovery Geometry (Failure Pattern)

Recovery can fail if:

- Load remains too high  
- Curvature cannot flatten  
- Invariants cannot reload  
- Identity boundaries remain deformed  
- Legality masks cannot re‑activate  

Failure produces **recovery geometry**, which DSLO routes into:

- Fallback Mode  
- Quarantine Mode  
- Regeneration pathways  

Recovery failure is not random; it produces a **lawful geometric signature**.
