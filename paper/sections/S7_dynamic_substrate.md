# S7 — Dynamic Human Substrate  
## Semantic Edition (DSLO Human Container v0.5)

S7 describes the dynamic behavior of human meaning systems: how interpretive
states evolve over time, how new conditions are integrated, how stability is
maintained, and how recovery occurs when drift or load approach critical
thresholds. Whereas Sections S1–S6 define the relational invariants, boundaries,
drift dynamics, load pressures, cross-scale coupling, and collapse modes, S7
defines the lawful update process that governs how human meaning changes.

The dynamic human substrate is the system’s execution layer. It performs
interpretive transitions, while all previous layers constrain, shape, or evaluate
the legality of those transitions.

---

## S7.1 State Representation

A dynamic human state is represented as a structured collection of relational
quantities:

> A human state contains the individual’s current coherence, drift, identity
> boundaries, role structure, legality status, and active interpretive regions.

Specifically, a human state includes:

- **coherence profile** — clarity and stability of human interpretation  
- **drift signature** — indicators of deviation from human invariants  
- **identity boundaries** — current configuration of selfhood regions  
- **role structure** — relationships among active roles and contexts  
- **legality status** — evaluation of invariant-preserving interpretive legality  
- **region configuration** — active interpretive regions and cross-scale context  

The structure of the human state is fixed, but its values evolve lawfully over
time.

---

## S7.2 The Lawful Update Process

The dynamic human substrate evolves according to a lawful update process:

> A new human state is produced by integrating the previous state, the incoming
> condition, and the active context—provided all human invariants remain
> satisfied.

The update is lawful only if:

- identity and meaning invariants are preserved  
- drift remains within recoverable bounds  
- load remains within stable or critical envelopes  
- cross-scale coupling remains coherent  
- collapse is not triggered  
- interpretive transitions remain human-legal  

If any condition fails, the update halts and transitions into recovery mode.

---

## S7.3 Constrained Evolution

All dynamic human evolution must follow lawful relational transitions. These
transitions must:

- preserve identity boundaries  
- maintain role coherence  
- avoid coherence discontinuities  
- keep drift within recoverable limits  
- keep load below collapse thresholds  
- satisfy legality rules across scales  

The dynamic substrate enforces that every human transition is relationally valid.

---

## S7.4 Context-Integrated Updates

Context modulates the update process. A context is integrated only if it
satisfies human legality.

- If context is lawful, it shapes the update.  
- If context is unlawful, it is treated as observational only.

Context integration must satisfy:

- identity-boundary compatibility  
- coherence continuity  
- drift bounds  
- role stability  
- legality rules  
- cross-scale consistency  

Context therefore acts as a stabilizing or destabilizing influence depending on
its relational structure.

---

## S7.5 Drift and Coherence Updates

Drift and coherence are updated as part of each dynamic human transition.

- **Drift Update** — evaluates how new conditions shift the individual relative
  to the invariant-preserving trajectory  
- **Coherence Update** — recalculates clarity and interpretive stability across
  roles and contexts  

Both updates must remain:

- continuous  
- bounded  
- invariant-preserving  
- legality-consistent  

If drift or coherence exceed collapse thresholds, the dynamic substrate halts
(Section S6).

---

## S7.6 Halt Conditions

The dynamic human substrate halts when:

- drift exceeds irrecoverable bounds  
- load exceeds collapse limits  
- cross-scale legality fails  
- collapse signatures appear  
- human legality is violated  

Upon halt, the system transitions into recovery mode.

---

## S7.7 Recovery Mode

Recovery restores human invariants and stabilizes interpretation. Recovery is
permitted only if:

- drift remains within the recoverable window  
- identity boundaries can be restored  
- coherence can be re-established  
- legality rules can be satisfied  
- cross-scale consistency can be regained  

Recovery transitions the system back into stable or critical mode.

---

## S7.8 Completion of the Update Cycle

A dynamic human update cycle completes when:

- human invariants are preserved  
- interpretive transitions remain lawful  
- cross-scale legality is satisfied  

At this point:

- the new human state becomes the active state  
- constraints from Sections S1–S6 re-engage  
- the system continues lawful human evolution  

---

## S7.9 Synthesis: Human Meaning + Human Condition

S7 couples human meaning with human condition:

### Meaning–Condition Coupling  
The human invariants provide the meaning substrate; lived experience provides the
condition substrate. A coupling event is the alignment point.

### Alignment as Structural Stability  
A human system is aligned when its lived condition matches its invariant meaning
geometry.

### Coupling Event Output  
A coupling event becomes a Human Meaning & Drift Report:

- what the human invariants mean  
- how roles and contexts extend that meaning  
- how the system is performing against those meanings  
- where drift is accumulating  
- where invariants remain strong  

### Forward Posture  
Maintain invariants.  
Reduce drift.  
Reaffirm human meaning.

S7 therefore completes the human substrate: a lawful, invariant-preserving system
that evolves coherently across time.
