# S1 — Human Identity & Signal Layer  
## Semantic Edition (DSLO Human Container v0.5)

S1 establishes the foundational relational conditions required for coherent human
interpretation. It introduces the basic structures that allow a human meaning
system to maintain identity, orientation, and coherence across shifting social,
digital, and technological contexts. These structures form the initial layer of
the human substrate and constrain all lawful interpretive transitions across
Sections S1–S7.

S1 is not domain-specific. It applies uniformly to individuals, groups,
communities, and human-in-digital systems. Its purpose is to define the stable
relational boundaries and meaning-preserving structures on which all subsequent
human layers operate.

---

## S1.1 Substrate Declaration

The human substrate begins by identifying the core relational conditions that
govern coherent change across systems that possess:

- a stable human identity boundary  
- a meaning-preserving role and context structure  
- a coherent interpretive environment  
- a legality condition governing human interpretive transitions  

These conditions are not metaphors or abstractions. They describe the lawful
relational structure that supports stable human meaning in any environment where
signals, roles, and contexts evolve.

---

## S1.2 Human Identity Boundary

The human substrate enforces the **Identity Invariant**:

> A human meaning system remains itself across interpretive transitions unless
> undergoing a lawful identity update.

Human identity boundaries are minimal relational structures that:

- define the individual’s stable region of selfhood  
- prevent drift across incompatible roles or contexts  
- constrain transitions that would fragment identity  
- anchor the human meaning system within its interpretive environment  

Identity boundaries are not labels; they are relational constraints governing
admissible human transitions.

---

## S1.3 Human Meaning Invariant

The human substrate enforces the **Meaning Invariant**:

> Human meaning remains stable when role-legality is preserved across
> transitions.

Human meaning is defined as **role-preserving interpretive legality**, not
preference, emotion, or subjective interpretation. The Meaning Invariant ensures
that:

- interpretive sequences remain coherent  
- role assignments remain stable  
- transitions do not induce human drift  
- the individual’s functional identity is preserved  

Meaning is therefore a relational property of lawful human interpretive action.

---

## S1.4 Human Interpretive Environment

All human interpretive dynamics occur within a structured environment defined by:

- **adjacency relations** — which signals can meaningfully interact  
- **clarity conditions** — how coherence is maintained  
- **load patterns** — how compensatory pressures accumulate under drift  
- **boundary structures** — how identity and legality are preserved  

Every lawful human transition must respect these relational constraints. No
transition may:

- violate adjacency conditions  
- destabilize clarity  
- cross identity boundaries illegally  
- induce irreversible human drift  

The interpretive environment is the human meaning system’s lawful space of
coherence.

---

## S1.5 Human Legality Invariant

The human substrate enforces a **Legality Invariant** across:

- Local Layer (individual coherence)  
- Structural Layer (lawful interpretive transitions)  
- Social Layer (group and environmental stability)  

This invariant ensures that:

- local coherence implies structural coherence  
- violations cannot propagate across layers  
- drift remains bounded across scales  
- human interpretive sequences remain stable  

The Legality Invariant binds S1–S7 into a unified human relational discipline.

---

## S1.6 Human Substrate Summary

S1 defines the human meaning system as a substrate with:

- **Substrate Identity:** a stable human meaning boundary  
- **Substrate Geometry:** the human invariants (Boundary, Coherence, Continuity,
  Fallback, Signal Integrity, Orientation)  
- **Substrate Vectors:** lawful interpretive transitions across roles, contexts,
  and environments  
- **Substrate Constraints:** adjacency, clarity, load, and boundary conditions
  that preserve coherence  

These structures form the human substrate’s foundational meaning layer and
constrain all lawful transitions across the human system.
