## Semantic Edition (DSLO Human Container v0.5)

S2 formalizes the human boundary structure and the lawful role dynamics that
govern coherent human interpretation. Whereas S1 establishes human identity and
meaning as invariants, S2 explains how those invariants are maintained through
boundary conditions, role constraints, legality rules, and drift limits. S2 is
the human substrate’s constraint layer, ensuring that interpretive transitions
remain identity-stable, meaning-preserving, and globally coherent across

## S2.1 Human Identity Invariant Formalization

The Human Identity Invariant constrains how the individual’s identity boundary
behaves under interpretive change. It formalizes the lawful transitions permitted
when human meaning shifts occur and prohibits transitions that would fragment or
collapse identity.

### Formal Definition

> The human identity boundary must remain coherent and non-permeable across
> interpretive transitions unless undergoing a lawful identity update.

### Boundary Conditions

Identity boundaries enforce the following relational constraints:

- identity regions must remain stable and interpretable  
- transitions may not induce fragmentation of selfhood  
- identity updates must follow lawful human-role pathways  
- identity drift must remain within recoverable limits  
- cross-context interactions must satisfy human legality  

The Human Identity Invariant defines the substrate’s lawful region of human
selfhood.

## S2.2 Human Meaning Invariant Formalization

change. It defines the lawful preservation of role structure across all human

> transitions.

### Role Preservation

Human meaning remains stable when:

- human roles remain interpretable across transitions  
- role categories remain stable across contexts  
- transitions do not induce role collapse or ambiguity  
- functional human meaning remains coherent across S1–S7  

### Perceptual / Functional Invariance

Human meaning must remain invariant across:

- perceptual changes in environment  
- representational changes in digital or social signals  
- interpretive updates across roles  
- cross-layer projections from local to social scale  

Meaning is therefore a relational property of lawful human interpretive action.

## S2.5 Human Drift Envelope

The Human Drift Envelope defines the lawful bounds of deviation permitted during
interpretive change.

### Definition of Drift

Human drift is the deviation of identity or meaning from its invariant-preserving
trajectory.

### Drift Bounds

- **recoverable drift** — correctable through lawful human transitions  
- **collapse threshold** — drift triggering failure detection  
- **irrecoverable drift** — drift requiring a halt condition  

### Drift Halt Triggers

A halt is triggered when:

- drift exceeds irrecoverable bounds  
- identity boundary collapse is detected  
- coherence discontinuity occurs  
- legality conditions are violated  

The Drift Envelope is the human substrate’s final guardrail against collapse.
