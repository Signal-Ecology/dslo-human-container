# Appendix G — Substrate Geometry Diagrams  
## Semantic Edition (DSLO Human Container v0.5)

This appendix provides the canonical geometric objects used throughout the
substrate. Each diagram is expressed here in semantic-geometric form rather than
TikZ. These objects constitute the visual layer of the substrate and correspond
to the meaning manifold, operator algebra, species geometry, and collapse
boundaries defined in Sections S1–S7 and Appendices A–F.

## G.1 Manifold Maps (Semantic Description)

The meaning manifold is represented as a compact semantic space with:

- a **manifold boundary** defining collapse thresholds  
- an **identity region** representing stable selfhood  
- a **context anchor** used for local semantic contraction  
- a **lawful contraction-governed trajectory** representing invariant-preserving
  movement through the manifold  

### Semantic Geometry  
- The manifold is a closed, bounded semantic region.  
- The identity region is a lawful subregion with stable boundaries.  
- The context anchor defines the local neighborhood for contraction.  
- Lawful trajectories move inward toward the anchor, reducing semantic distance
  and preserving curvature.

This map encodes:

- compact manifold geometry (Appendix A)  
- identity-region topology (Appendix C)  
- contraction-governed operator action (Appendix B)  
- lawful drift-bounded trajectories (Sections S3–S4)

## G.2 Operator Flow Diagrams (Semantic Description)

Operator flow is represented as:

- a **lawful transition** from state *u* to state *v*  
- an **illegal transition** from state *u* to a collapse region  
- a **context anchor** validating contraction  
- a **curvature variance check** ensuring legality  

### Semantic Geometry  
- Lawful transitions contract semantic distance relative to the anchor.  
- Illegal transitions violate curvature bounds or invariant constraints.  
- The collapse region represents semantic entropy, curvature decay, or boundary
  violation.

This diagram encodes:

- lawful operator transitions (IRT, IPU, GBS, LRP)  
- contraction validation (Appendix B)  
- invariant preservation (Appendix C)  
- rejection of illegal transitions (Sections S2.4, S7.2)

## G.4 Collapse Boundary Visualization (Semantic Description)

Collapse geometry is represented as:

- a **manifold boundary** defining collapse thresholds  
- a **lawful trajectory** that preserves curvature and legality  
- a **collapse trajectory** that violates curvature or boundary constraints  
- **curvature markers** indicating stability vs collapse proximity  

### Semantic Geometry  
- Lawful trajectories remain inside the manifold and preserve curvature.  
- Collapse trajectories approach or cross the manifold boundary.  
- Curvature decay indicates drift amplification or recursive self-ingestion.  
- Collapse occurs when curvature approaches zero or boundary conditions fail.

This diagram encodes:

- lawful contraction-governed trajectories (Sections S3–S4)  
- curvature decay under autophagy or drift amplification (Appendix E)  
- finite-time collapse behavior (Section S6.1)  
- boundary violation under illegal recursion (Section S6.3)  
- DSUP halt-and-recover enforcement (Sections S7.6–S7.7)
