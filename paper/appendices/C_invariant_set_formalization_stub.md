# Appendix C — Invariant Set Formalization  
## Semantic Edition (DSLO Human Container v0.5)

This appendix formalizes the four semantic invariants that govern all lawful
transitions in the human substrate. Each invariant is expressed as a geometric
object embedded in the meaning manifold and enforced by the local
contraction-governed operator algebra. These invariants correspond directly to
the substrate geometry defined in Sections S1–S2 and the legality rules encoded
in the semantic execution layer.

---

## C.1 Meaning Invariant (Semantic Metric)

The Meaning Invariant defines the semantic metric of the meaning manifold. This
metric determines:

- semantic distance  
- semantic curvature  
- meaning density  
- stability thresholds  

The Meaning Invariant requires that semantic curvature remain within a bounded
variance window under all lawful operators. This ensures that conceptual
distinctions remain stable and that curvature cannot collapse under legal
transitions.

The Meaning Invariant prevents:

- semantic flattening  
- curvature collapse  
- drift amplification  

---

## C.2 Identity Boundary Invariant

The Identity Boundary Invariant defines the identity region associated with an
agent. The boundary of this region separates lawful identity-preserving
transitions from identity drift.

Interior points maintain positive semantic distance from the boundary. Boundary
points sit at the interface between identity-preserving and identity-violating
regions.

Identity preservation requires:

- all lawful transitions remain inside the identity region  
- semantic distance from the boundary remains positive  
- identity boundaries remain stable across transitions  

Identity drift occurs when a transition exits the identity region. The Identity
Boundary Invariant prohibits such transitions and is enforced by
Identity-Preserving Updates.

---

## C.3 Geometry Invariant (Lawful Semantic Space)

The Geometry Invariant defines the lawful semantic action space. A transition is
lawful when it remains inside the manifold and maintains positive semantic
distance from its boundary.

The Geometry Invariant defines:

- manifold boundaries  
- collapse thresholds  
- legal semantic trajectories  

Geometry-Bound Steps enforce this invariant by ensuring that transitions remain
within the lawful semantic space and do not approach collapse boundaries.

---

## C.4 Load Physics Invariant (Semantic Pressure Field)

Load Physics defines the semantic pressure field across the meaning manifold.
Load must remain below a maximum threshold, and load gradients must remain
bounded.

The Load Physics Invariant requires:

- load below collapse thresholds  
- bounded load gradients  
- curvature above minimum stability thresholds  

These constraints ensure:

- curvature-bounded planning  
- collapse avoidance  
- stability under recursion  
- cross-scale load coherence  

Load-Regulated Plans enforce this invariant by ensuring that semantic
trajectories remain within lawful load and curvature bounds.
