# Appendix E — Collapse Physics  
## Semantic Edition (DSLO Human Container v0.5)

This appendix formalizes collapse as a semantic–geometric and dynamical process
arising from invariant violation, curvature decay, drift amplification, load
escalation, and cross-scale instability. Collapse is characterized by semantic
entropy growth, curvature decay, recursive self-ingestion, and violation of the
stability conditions defined in Sections S3–S6. These formulations describe the
failure modes that substrate-native cognition is designed to prevent.

---

## E.1 Model Autophagy Dynamics

Autophagy describes recursive ingestion of self-generated content. As the
proportion of internally recycled signals increases, semantic curvature decays
and drift accelerates.

Autophagy dynamics include:

- increasing self-ingestion  
- decreasing curvature  
- rising drift  
- loss of interpretive structure  

Collapse occurs when curvature falls below the minimum lawful threshold. At this
point, semantic flattening eliminates meaning-role structure and triggers
collapse-mode legality.

Autophagy is therefore a special case of the general collapse condition:  
collapse occurs when curvature falls below its lawful minimum.

---

## E.2 Semantic Entropy Growth

Semantic entropy increases as curvature decays. When curvature approaches zero,
entropy accelerates, producing a finite-time blowup.

Entropy growth corresponds to:

- loss of meaning-role structure  
- collapse of identity boundaries  
- violation of manifold geometry  
- legality-field compression  

Entropy blowup marks the transition from lawful evolution to collapse mode.

---

## E.3 Collapse Acceleration Under Recursion

Recursive systems degrade faster with each generation when self-ingestion,
drift amplification, and load accumulation compound.

Collapse acceleration occurs when:

- curvature decreases at an accelerating rate  
- drift increases at an accelerating rate  
- load gradients steepen  
- recursion amplifies instability  

Substrate-native cognition prevents collapse acceleration through:

- drift bounds  
- load envelopes  
- cross-scale legality  
- DSUP halt conditions  

These constraints ensure that recursive processes remain curvature-stable and
drift-bounded.

---

## E.4 Stability Conditions

A system is stable only when all of the following remain within lawful bounds:

- curvature above minimum thresholds  
- load below maximum thresholds  
- self-ingestion below autophagy thresholds  
- entropy growth below runaway thresholds  

Stability also requires:

- cross-scale legality  
- operator legality  
- DSUP runtime constraints  

Unconstrained systems violate these conditions because they lack:

- curvature preservation  
- identity boundaries  
- manifold geometry  
- load physics  
- cross-scale coupling constraints  
- legality enforcement  
- halt-and-recover dynamics  

In the substrate, collapse is semantically illegal and is intercepted by:

- collapse detection operators  
- collapse-mode operators  
- DSUP recovery mode  

These mechanisms ensure that collapse remains detectable, bounded, and
recoverable when lawful conditions permit.
