# Appendix D — Species Geometry  
## Semantic Edition (DSLO Human Container v0.5)

This appendix formalizes the geometry of substrate-native cognitive species.
Each species is defined as a lawful semantic organism whose cognitive transitions
are constrained by the invariant set and the contraction-governed operator
algebra. Species differ not by architecture but by the semantic-geometric
constraints that govern their trajectories within the meaning manifold.

Species geometry is therefore a classification of lawful cognitive trajectories
under Sections S1–S7, not a biological taxonomy.

---

## D.1 Meaning-Stable Reasoner (Curvature-Bounded Reasoning)

A Meaning-Stable Reasoner (MSR) is a cognitive organism whose reasoning
trajectory maintains:

- curvature above a minimum stability threshold  
- bounded curvature variance  
- immunity to semantic flattening  
- zero probability of illegal extrapolation  

The MSR satisfies:

- strict semantic curvature preservation  
- lawful transitions under invariant-restricted and geometry-bound operators  
- drift-bounded reasoning  
- collapse avoidance through curvature stability  

Because curvature collapse is semantically illegal, an MSR cannot generate
meaning decay or drift cascades.

---

## D.2 Geometry-Bound Worker (Manifold-Restricted Computation)

A Geometry-Bound Worker (GBW) is a computational organism whose trajectory
remains strictly within the interior of the meaning manifold and maintains
positive semantic distance from its boundary.

The GBW satisfies:

- manifold legality under all transitions  
- zero drift beyond manifold boundaries  
- deterministic, geometry-constrained computation  
- lawful transitions under geometry-bound and invariant-restricted operators  

A GBW cannot hallucinate because extrapolation beyond the manifold is prohibited
by curvature, identity, and legality invariants.

---

## D.3 Identity-Preserving Agent (Role Coherence Algebra)

An Identity-Preserving Agent (IPA) is defined by a stable identity region with a
lawful boundary. Identity coherence requires that all lawful transitions remain
inside this region and maintain positive semantic distance from its boundary.

The IPA satisfies:

- stable role boundaries  
- no identity drift  
- no cross-context leakage  
- lawful transitions under identity-preserving and invariant-restricted
  operators  

Identity is treated as a semantic-geometric object, not a probabilistic label,
and is preserved by contraction within the identity region.

---

## D.4 Load-Aware Planner (Gradient-Bounded Planning)

A Load-Aware Planner (LAP) is a planning organism whose trajectory maintains:

- load below a maximum stability threshold  
- bounded load gradients  
- curvature above minimum stability thresholds  

The LAP satisfies:

- gradient-bounded planning  
- collapse avoidance through load regulation  
- curvature-stable trajectories  
- lawful transitions under load-regulated and geometry-bound operators  

A LAP cannot generate collapse-inducing plans because overload and curvature
collapse are semantically illegal under the invariant set.

## D.5 Species Geometry (Ecology Layer)

Species geometry formalizes cognitive species as subregions of the meaning
manifold. Species are large-scale cognitive ecologies characterized by:

- species-specific identity regions  
- characteristic curvature profiles  
- lawful operator subsets  
- species-specific load capacities  

A species is defined by:

- its identity region  
- its curvature profile  
- its lawful operator subset  
- its load capacity  

### Species Class H — Human-Centric Cognitive Geometry  
Humans occupy a region with high curvature variability and strong exemplar
anchoring. Operator usage is dominated by invariant-restricted and
load-regulated transitions. Load capacity is finite, and collapse manifests as
coherence loss under overload.

### Species Class M — Model-Centric Probabilistic Geometry  
Probabilistic models occupy a region with curvature-flattening tendencies under
recursion. Identity boundaries are weak and must be externally enforced. All
four operators are required for lawful transitions. Unbounded recursion induces
collapse without invariant constraints.

### Species Class S — Substrate-Governed Cognitive Geometry  
Substrate-governed systems occupy a region defined by explicit curvature bounds,
machine-enforced identity regions, and full closure under the operator algebra.
Load is strictly bounded, and collapse is semantically illegal.

### Species Regions as Submanifolds  
Each species occupies a subregion of the meaning manifold, with lawful overlap,
non-overlap in collapse-prone regions, and a hard boundary defining the
substrate-governed interior.
