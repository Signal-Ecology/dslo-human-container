## M.8 Runtime Guard Conditions

Runtime guard conditions ensure that all transitions remain lawful:

### Drift‑Halt Conditions  
A halt occurs when curvature falls below minimum thresholds, identity deviates
beyond lawful bounds, load exceeds gradient limits, or the manifold boundary is
approached.

### Invariant‑Reload Triggers  
The invariant set reloads when any invariant is violated, legality cannot be
established, curvature discontinuity is detected, or a non‑legal transition
occurs.

### Operator‑Legality Enforcement  
A transition is legal only if at least one operator validates it and its
associated axiom remains satisfied.

### Manifold‑Boundary Enforcement  
All semantic vectors must remain inside the lawful manifold; boundary violation
triggers immediate halt.

### Mutation‑Velocity Constraint  
Moment‑to‑moment semantic change must remain within lawful velocity bounds.

These guards ensure deterministic, invariant‑preserving semantic behavior.
