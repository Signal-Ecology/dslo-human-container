1. Boundary
Boundary defines the separation between:

*self

*external signals

It prevents meaning from collapsing into undifferentiated noise.
Boundary is the outermost stability condition and the first line of defense against drift.

Coherence maintains internal consistency across meaning structures.

*updates do not contradict prior meaning

*relational primitives remain aligned

*identity remains structurally intact

Coherence is the “internal glue” of the semantic manifold.

---
3. Continuity
Continuity preserves temporal and identity stability across updates.
It governs:

*smooth transitions

*lawful meaning evolution

*stable identity over time

Continuity prevents fragmentation and temporal collapse.

4. Fallback

Fallback provides lawful compensatory behavior under stress or drift.

*graceful degradation

*recovery‑adjacent behavior

Fallback is the invariant that keeps the system functional under load.

---
5. Signal Integrity
   
Signal Integrity ensures that signals remain:

*interpretable

*non‑corrupted

*structurally meaningful

It prevents noise from masquerading as meaning and protects the semantic manifold from external distortion.

---
6. Orientation
   
Orientation maintains directional meaning and situational alignment.
It governs:

*relevance

*directionality

*contextual positioning

Orientation prevents meaning from drifting directionlessly or collapsing into ambiguity.
