Overview

The Load Map diagram visualizes how compensatory load accumulates within the DSLO Human‑Container as meaning drifts away from invariant alignment. Load is not “stress” in the psychological sense — it is a semantic physics quantity representing the system’s effort to maintain coherence under deformation.

Load is the second stage of instability, following drift and preceding collapse.

This map shows load gradients, stress envelopes, compensatory load buildup, load‑induced drift, and the curvature conditions that accelerate collapse.

Load gradients represent the direction and intensity of compensatory load accumulation.

*how drift contributes to load formation

Load gradients always point toward regions of increasing curvature stress.
